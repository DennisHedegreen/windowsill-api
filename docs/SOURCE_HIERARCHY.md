# Source Hierarchy

Windowsill plant data should prefer sources that explain their own basis.

AI tools may help find, compare and summarize sources, but AI output is not a source.

## 1. Botanical Authority

Use for:

- accepted Latin names
- families
- genera
- synonyms
- native, introduced or cultivated range

Examples:

- Kew Plants of the World Online
- GRIN / USDA Germplasm Resources Information Network
- national botanical databases
- herbarium references
- botanical garden references

## 2. Horticultural Authority

Use for:

- growing conditions
- container suitability
- sun needs
- propagation
- hardiness
- practical cultivation

Examples:

- RHS
- university extension services
- botanical gardens
- specialist horticultural societies

## 3. Crop and Agronomy Sources

Use for:

- food crops
- traditional crops
- field crops
- tuber crops
- grain crops
- yield and season behaviour

Examples:

- FAO
- CGIAR / CIP
- university agronomy papers
- agricultural extension PDFs

## 4. Cultural and Culinary Sources

Use for:

- local names
- food use
- regional significance
- culinary tradition

These sources are valuable, but they should not override botanical, horticultural or safety evidence.

## 5. Personal Observation

Personal observation is valuable, especially for windowsill and balcony behaviour.

It can support:

- pot size
- local climate
- orientation
- sowing date
- germination
- first harvest
- failure notes
- microclimate observations

It should not be the only source for:

- toxicity
- native range
- hardiness
- broad climate limits
- edible safety claims
