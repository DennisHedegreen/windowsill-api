# Source registry — WSL-0001 Genovese Basil

Status: audit draft  
Plant: Genovese Basil  
Candidate ID: WSL-0001  
Folder: `research-packs/WSL-0001-genovese-basil/`

## Published / institutional sources

### 1. Plants of the World Online — Kew Science
URL: https://powo.science.kew.org/taxon/urn:lsid:ipni.org:names:452874-1

Supports:
- `Ocimum basilicum L.` is an accepted species name.
- Species-level identity for sweet basil.
- Broad statement that the species is used for food.
- Native range and growth form at species level.

Does not support:
- That every commercial product labelled "Genovese Basil" is one genetically identical cultivar.
- Exact Windowsill model temperatures or container suitability.

### 2. Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew — Windowsill basil growing leaflet
URL: https://www.kew.org/sites/default/files/2023-05/13494_Basil_growing_project_2pp_A5_Leaflet_Web%20acc_ISD_AW.pdf

Supports:
- Basil belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae.
- Basil thrives in warm weather with full sun, air circulation and moisture.
- Basil is treated as an annual in the UK/temperate context.
- Basil can be grown on a sunny windowsill.
- Basil leaves can be eaten raw or added to cooked food.
- Basil flowers can also be eaten.

Does not support:
- Exact Genovese-specific days to maturity.
- Exact numeric min/max temperature model values.

### 3. University of Minnesota Extension — Growing basil in home gardens
URL: https://extension.umn.edu/vegetables/growing-basil

Supports:
- Common/sweet basil is `Ocimum basilicum`.
- Basil is a tender annual used for aromatic leaves.
- Basil requires 6–8 hours of bright light and well-drained soil.
- Seeds germinate in about 5–7 days under suitable conditions.
- Container plants dry out faster and indoor plants may need strong/artificial light.

Does not support:
- Genovese cultivar identity by itself.
- Essential-oil or medicinal safety.

### 4. Iowa State University Extension — Growing Basil in the Home Garden
URL: https://yardandgarden.extension.iastate.edu/how-to/growing-basil-home-garden

Supports:
- Basil is `Ocimum basilicum` and a member of the mint family.
- Basil is frost-sensitive and does not tolerate cold temperatures.
- Direct outdoor sowing should wait until nights are consistently above about 50°F / 10°C.
- Basil grows well in containers and can be grown indoors in a sunny window.
- Harvesting before seed formation supports better flavour and ongoing harvest.

Does not support:
- Exact upper heat limit.
- Exact hardiness-zone modelling.

### 5. US Basil Consortium / Rutgers — Growing Basil in a Commercial Setting
URL: https://usbasilconsortium.rutgers.edu/grower-research-resources/grower-resources/growing-basil-in-a-commercial-setting/

Supports:
- Basil needs full sunlight and at least six hours of direct sunlight per day.
- Optimal growth temperature range is given as 70–90°F / 21–32°C.
- Basil prefers fertile, well-drained soil with good moisture retention.

Does not support:
- Windowsill performance under weak winter light.
- Genovese-specific timing for the base cultivar.

### 6. De Bolster — Basilicum 'Genovese'
URL: https://www.bolster.nl/basilicum-genovese/p102

Supports:
- Commercial Genovese basil seed sold as `Ocimum basilicum`.
- Genovese basil described as annual, low-growing, medium-leaved and strongly aromatic.
- Requires water, light, warmth and nutrition.
- Plant spacing listed as 20 × 20 cm.
- Plant height listed as 20–50 cm.
- Site/light listed as sun.
- Seed type listed as fixed/open variety (`zaadvast ras`).
- Multiple harvest after regrowth is possible if cut correctly.

Does not support:
- Every market strain sold as Genovese being open-pollinated.
- Scientific cultivar registration.

### 7. Johnny's Selected Seeds — Genovese Basil Seed
URL: https://www.johnnyseeds.com/herbs/basil/genovese-basil/genovese-basil-seed-911.html

Supports:
- Genovese basil as a seed product with 68 days to maturity from seeding date for herbs.
- Germination range of 5–10 days at 65–70°F / 18–21°C.
- Indoor sowing six weeks before transplanting and full growing information.

Does not support:
- Exact Windowsill `grow_time_weeks` as a universal value.
- Regional performance outside the seed supplier's assumptions.

### 8. PanAmerican Seed — Everleaf Genovese Basil
URL: https://www.panamseed.com/Products/009100001036966/basil-everleaf-genovese/

Supports:
- A Genovese-type basil can be suitable for pots/containers.
- A specific compact Genovese cultivar has 30–45 days to maturity from transplant.

Does not support:
- Standard Genovese basil generally being compact or late flowering.
- Applying 30–45 days from transplant to all Genovese basil entries without uncertainty.

### 9. BfR — Alkenylbenzenes in food: How large is the health risk?
URL: https://www.bfr.bund.de/cm/349/alkenylbenzenes-in-food-how-large-is-the-health-risk.pdf

Supports:
- Basil may naturally contain alkenylbenzenes.
- Basil-containing pesto and plant-based supplements may contain high amounts of alkenylbenzenes.
- Estragole, methyleugenol and safrole have mutagenic/carcinogenic concern in animal studies and are regulated when added as flavouring substances.

Does not support:
- That ordinary culinary basil leaves are unsafe in normal food use.
- Any exact dose limit for homegrown basil leaves.

### 10. Wageningen University & Research portal — FEMA GRAS assessment
URL: https://research.wur.nl/en/publications/fema-gras-assessment-of-derivatives-of-basil-nutmeg-parsley-tarra/

Supports:
- Basil-derived natural flavour complexes were assessed in relation to allylalkoxybenzene constituents.
- The assessment scope explicitly concerns intended flavour use and does not include dietary supplements or other non-food uses.

Does not support:
- Safety of basil essential oil as a supplement.
- Home medicinal dosing.
